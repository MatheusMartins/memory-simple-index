Allocation

Memory allocation is the process of dedicating a deliberate amount of space to a specific task.

Static allocated memory can be referred to as static, fixed, compile-time or stack. Static allocation is safe because the size and lifetime of the memory is known at compile time, reducing the risk of errors such as memory leaks or corruption.
